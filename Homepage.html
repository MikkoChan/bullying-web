Types of Bullying

Physical Bullying

Physical bullying involves physical attacks such as hitting, kicking, or pushing. It's one of the most visible and straightforward forms of bullying.

Example: Watch it on Youtube.

Verbal Bullying

Verbal bullying includes teasing, name-calling, inappropriate sexual comments, and taunting. It can be very damaging to the victim's mental health.

Example: Watch it on Youtube.

Relational Bullying

Also known as social bullying, it involves hurting someone's reputation or relationships, like spreading rumors or deliberately excluding someone from a group.

Example: Watch it on Youtube.

Cyber Bullying

Cyber bullying occurs online and includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else, causing embarrassment or humiliation.

Example: Watch it on Youtube.

Sexual Bullying

Sexual bullying involves harmful, humiliating actions that target a person sexually. Examples include sexual name-calling, crude comments, vulgar gestures, or unconsented touching.

Example: Watch it on Youtube.

Prejudicial Bullying

This type of bullying arises from prejudices people may have toward people of different races, religions, or other groups. It can overlap with other types of bullying and lead to severe discrimination.

Legal Framework Against Bullying

Under DepEd Order No. 55 s. 2013 or the Implementing Rules and Regulations of Republic Act (RA) No. 10627 or the Anti-Bullying Act of 2013, bullying refers to "any severe, or repeated use by one or more students of a written, verbal or electronic expression, or a physical act or gesture, or any combination thereof, directed at another student that has the effect of actually causing or placing the latter in reasonable fear of physical or emotional harm or damage to his property, creating a hostile environment at school for the other student; infringing on the rights of another student at school, or materially and substantially disrupting the education process or the orderly operation of a school."

Experts said students who experience bullying are seen to be at increased risk for depression, anxiety, sleep difficulties, and lower academic achievement.

Under RA 10627, any school employee who commits bullying shall be punished by a fine of not less than PHP50,000 but not more than PHP100,000, at least six months but not more than one year of imprisonment, or both fine and imprisonment. (PNA)

About Our Organization

At the Manolo Fortich National High School, our unwavering dedication lies in cultivating an environment that is not only safe and respectful but also profoundly supportive for every individual within our community. We understand the vital role that a nurturing educational environment plays in shaping the academic, social, and emotional well-being of our students. Through our commitment to fostering inclusivity, promoting empathy, and upholding the principles of respect and understanding, we strive to create a school culture where every student feels valued, heard, and empowered to thrive. Our comprehensive approach encompasses not only academic excellence but also prioritizes the holistic development of each student, emphasizing the importance of empathy, collaboration, and resilience. Through initiatives such as peer mentoring programs, anti-bullying campaigns, and community outreach efforts, we endeavor to instill in our students the values of compassion, integrity, and social responsibility. By working collaboratively with students, parents, educators, and community stakeholders, we aim to create an educational environment that serves as a beacon of inclusivity, equity, and excellence for all.

Our Commitment

We offer a comprehensive array of resources, support services, and informational materials geared towards empowering individuals to comprehend and navigate the multifaceted impacts of bullying. Our initiative is dedicated to fostering awareness, providing assistance, and fostering resilience in those affected by bullying, whether as targets, bystanders, or even perpetrators. Through a combination of educational materials, workshops, counseling services, and community outreach programs, we aim to shed light on the various forms of bullying, including verbal, physical, cyber, and social bullying, and equip individuals with the knowledge, skills, and resources needed to effectively recognize, address, and prevent bullying behavior. By fostering empathy, promoting healthy communication, and fostering a culture of inclusivity and respect, we strive to create safer and more supportive environments in schools, workplaces, and communities at large. Our ultimate goal is to cultivate a society where everyone feels valued, accepted, and empowered to stand up against bullying in all its forms.
